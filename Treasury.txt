1. CORE BUSINESS TERMINOLOGY & GLOSSARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The following glossary defines the authoritative business terminology and theoretical
formulas for the Treasury and Liquidity Management domains. These definitions
serve as semantic anchors for analytical agents.

TERM: Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)
- Definition: An enterprise metric measuring the adequacy of high-quality liquid 
  assets to survive a severe 30-day market stress scenario.
- Formula: LCR = Stock of HQLA / Total Net Cash Outflows over 30 days
- Impact: Governed by Basel III. A ratio below 100% indicates a severe breach.

TERM: Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)
- Definition: A regulatory metric measuring the availability of stable funding 
  relative to the liquidity profile of assets over a one-year horizon.
- Formula: NSFR = Available Stable Funding (ASF) / Required Stable Funding (RSF)
- Impact: Enforces structural balance sheet funding stability.

TERM: High-Quality Liquid Assets (HQLA)
- Definition: Unencumbered liquid assets that can be converted to cash easily 
  and immediately in private markets under stress.
- Sub-Classifications: Level 1 HQLA (no cap, 0% haircut), Level 2A HQLA (max 40%, 
  15% haircut), Level 2B HQLA (max 15%, 50% haircut).

TERM: Zero-Balance Account (ZBA)
- Definition: A corporate cash management account structure in which the balance 
  is automatically transferred to a master concentration account at the end of 
  each business day.
- Operational Rule: Day-end balance must always resolve to exactly zero.

TERM: Target-Balance Account (TBA)
- Definition: A corporate cash management account that maintains a predetermined 
  monetary floor. Any funds exceeding this target cushion are swept to the central pool.
- Operational Rule: Day-end balance must equal the defined cushion balance.

TERM: Nostro Account
- Definition: An account that AIM holds in foreign currency with a correspondent 
  bank in another country (literally "our account on your books").
- Scope: Used for clearing foreign currency payments and managing cross-border trade.

TERM: Vostro Account
- Definition: An account that a foreign correspondent bank holds on AIM's books 
  in our local currency (literally "your account on our books").
- Scope: Facilitates local settlement for offshore banking partners.

TERM: Secured Overnight Financing Rate (SOFR)
- Definition: A broad measure of the cost of borrowing cash overnight collateralized 
  by U.S. Treasury securities. Sourced from the repo market.
- Scope: Serves as the primary reference index for floating leg interest rate swaps.

TERM: Sterling Overnight Index Average (SONIA)
- Definition: The effective overnight interest rate paid by banks for unsecured 
  sterling transactions in the British market.
- Scope: Sourced as the reference rate for sterling-denominated derivative structures.

TERM: Euro Short-Term Rate (€STR)
- Definition: The reference rate reflecting the wholesale euro unsecured overnight 
  borrowing costs of banks located in the euro area.
- Scope: Used to discount and value euro-denominated cleared interest rate swaps.

TERM: Tokyo Overnight Average Rate (TONAR)
- Definition: The risk-free reference rate measuring unsecured overnight call rate 
  transactions within Japanese Yen financial markets.
- Scope: Primary floating rate benchmark utilized in Tokyo capital markets hedging.

TERM: Overnight Index Swap (OIS)
- Definition: An interest rate swap contract where the floating leg is tied to a 
  risk-free overnight reference rate (e.g., SOFR, SONIA, €STR).
- Scope: Minimizes counterparty credit risk and interest rate revaluation spikes.

TERM: Mark-to-Market (MTM)
- Definition: The valuation of a financial asset or derivative contract based on 
  the current prevailing market rates or closing prices.
- Scope: IRS and FX forward positions are revalued daily for collateral margin calls.

TERM: Variation Margin
- Definition: The daily collateral exchange between trading counterparties to 
  cover fluctuations in the Mark-to-Market valuation of cleared derivative contracts.
- SLA: Must settle prior to the daily clearing window cutoffs.

TERM: Initial Margin
- Definition: The collateral deposit required to open a derivative position or 
  participate in a cleared repo transaction to mitigate potential future exposure.
- Scope: Locked at the central clearinghouse (CCP) until contract termination.

TERM: Haircut
- Definition: The percentage discount applied to the market value of an asset 
  when utilized as collateral in a repo transaction or derivative margin pool.
- Purpose: Protects the lender against downward movements in asset valuation.

TERM: Tri-Party Repo
- Definition: A repurchase agreement in which a neutral third-party agent 
  (the custodian) manages collateral allocation, valuation, settlement, and margin.
- Scope: Minimizes counterparty risk through automated clearing matching.

TERM: Intraday Drawdown
- Definition: The maximum cumulative net negative cash flow occurring within a 
  single business day before standard end-of-day concentration sweeps are executed.
- Purpose: Used to monitor intraday settlement liquidity limits.

TERM: Foreign Exchange Exposure (FX Exposure)
- Definition: The risk of loss due to fluctuations in exchange rates when holding
  assets, liabilities, or derivatives in non-functional currencies.
- Scope: Tracked against the Net Asset Value (NAV) of regional corporate entities.

TERM: Variation Margin Dispute Resolution
- Definition: The protocol activated when counterparties disagree on daily closing 
  MTM valuations or applied haircut rates for collateral margins.
- Scope: Requires manual SME review and exchange of valuation calculations.

2. TREASURY CORE PRODUCTS CATALOG
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This section documents the specific financial and corporate structures managed
by the Treasury department, along with their logical constraints and scopes.

PRODUCT: Zero-Balance Concentration Sweep
- Purpose: Consolidate idle balances from subsidiary accounts into the central pool.
- Execution Frequency: Daily, post-standard clearing network window closes.
- Constraints: Requires functional SWIFT MT940 statement integration.

PRODUCT: Target-Balance Concentration Sweep
- Purpose: Preserve localized working capital while harvesting excess yield.
- Execution Frequency: Daily, at predetermined account cutoffs.
- Constraints: Requires active TBA floor value configuration in the account registry.

PRODUCT: Floating-to-Fixed Interest Rate Swap (IRS)
- Purpose: Hedge liabilities issued with floating-rate coupons against rate hikes.
- Underlyings: 3M SOFR, 3M SONIA, 3M EURIBOR, 3M TONAR.
- Constraints: Cleared strictly through approved Central Counterparties (CCPs).

PRODUCT: Sovereign Bond Repo Rollover
- Purpose: Maintain short-term funding liquidity by continuously pledging treasuries.
- Execution Frequency: Daily overnight rollover routing.
- Constraints: Collateral must meet Level 1 HQLA quality thresholds.

PRODUCT: Central Bank Discount Window Funding
- Purpose: Sourcing emergency liquidity during extreme market clearing failures.
- Execution Frequency: Discretionary/Emergency activation path.
- Constraints: Subject to high discount rates and institutional stigma limits.

PRODUCT: Cross-Currency Basis Swap
- Purpose: Convert liquid funding from functional currency to foreign settlement currency.
- Underlyings: Floating indexes across currency pairs (e.g., SOFR vs EURIBOR).
- Constraints: Pledges collateral margin at both currency clearing gateways.

PRODUCT: Tri-Party Collateral Substitution Sweep
- Purpose: Replace pledged securities in a repo trade with alternative eligible bonds.
- Execution Frequency: Ad-hoc as needed to release locked high-grade notes.
- Constraints: Alternative assets must satisfy the tri-party custodian haircut schedule.

PRODUCT: Overnight Repurchase Agreement (Repo)
- Purpose: Secure short-term cash by pledging high-grade liquid assets.
- Maturity: 1 Business Day (overnight rollover path).
- Constraints: Collateral must fall strictly under Level 1 HQLA parameters.

PRODUCT: Term Repurchase Agreement
- Purpose: Manage multi-day liquidity shortfalls with stable collateralized cash.
- Maturity: Pre-negotiated fixed term (typically 7 to 90 days).
- Constraints: Pledged assets are locked in tri-party custodian pools.

PRODUCT: Foreign Exchange Forward (FX Forward)
- Purpose: Hedge upcoming foreign currency settlement exposures on Nostro transfers.
- Settlement: Deliverable or non-deliverable at predetermined dates.
- Constraints: Total unhedged exposure must remain below defined regulatory caps.

Below is the definitive data dictionary for the 16 core tables in the Treasury
and Cash Management relational schemas. All Conversational BI generated SQL queries
must use these exact tables, column names, and data types.

TABLE: accounts
- Description: Stores primary master record information for all corporate bank accounts.
- Columns:
  * account_id (TEXT, PRIMARY KEY): Unique identifier for the corporate account.
  * account_number (TEXT, UNIQUE): Physical bank account number.
  * bank_name (TEXT): Correspondent or clearing bank name.
  * currency (TEXT): Functional ISO currency code (USD, EUR, GBP, etc.).
  * business_domain (TEXT): Operating domain (e.g., Treasury & Capital Management).
  * account_type (TEXT): ZBA_Child, ZBA_Master, TBA_Child, Operating, Nostro, Vostro.
  * balance_status (TEXT): Active, Frozen, Inactive.
  * routing_transit_number (TEXT): Domestic clearing transit routing code.
  * bic_swift_code (TEXT): Global SWIFT identification code.
  * legal_entity_owner (TEXT): Holding entity or regional corporate subsidiary.

TABLE: transactions
- Description: Logs real-time transactional movements across all corporate accounts.
- Columns:
  * transaction_id (TEXT, PRIMARY KEY): Unique transaction identifier.
  * account_id (TEXT, FOREIGN KEY REFERENCES accounts): Associated account.
  * amount (NUMERIC): Monies transferred (positive for credit, negative for debit).
  * value_date (DATE): Effective financial settlement date.
  * execution_timestamp (TIMESTAMP): System timestamp of execution.
  * payment_method (TEXT): Fedwire, CHIPS, Target2, SWIFT, SEPA.
  * transaction_status (TEXT): Settled, Pending, Failed, Exception.

  * hqla_level_2a (NUMERIC): Qualified corporate bonds and government debt.
  * hqla_level_2b (NUMERIC): Investment-grade corporate debt and equities.
  * net_outflows_30d (NUMERIC): Expected net cash outflows over a 30-day window.

4. LOGICAL JOIN CONDITIONS AND ENTITY RELATIONSHIPS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This section documents the explicit logical join maps required to traverse the 
Treasury relational databases. All SQL generation engines must adhere to these
joining bridges.

JOIN PATH: Accounts & Transactions (Core Statement Reconstruction)
- Relationship: One-to-Many
- Join Condition:
  `accounts.account_id = transactions.account_id`
- Purpose: Retrieve transaction flows and values for specific accounts.

JOIN PATH: Accounts & Nostro Balances (Nostro Reconciliation Flow)
- Relationship: One-to-Many
- Join Condition:
  `accounts.account_id = nostro_balances.account_id`
- Filtering Constraint: `accounts.account_type = 'Nostro'`
- Purpose: Identify foreign correspondent reconciliation exceptions.

JOIN PATH: Liquidity Sweeps & Executions (Concentration Harvesting Performance)
- Relationship: One-to-Many
- Join Condition:
  `liquidity_sweeps.sweep_id = sweep_executions.sweep_id`
- Purpose: Audit target balance vs zero balance sweep transfer efficiency.

JOIN PATH: Accounts & Liquidity Sweeps (Source and Target Resolution)
- Relationship: Many-to-One
- Join Condition (Source Account):
  `accounts.account_id = liquidity_sweeps.source_account_id`
- Join Condition (Target Account):
  `accounts.account_id = liquidity_sweeps.target_account_id`
- Purpose: Map geographic child accounts to their respective master aggregation nodes.

JOIN PATH: Repo Transactions & Investment Securities (Collateral Pledging Details)
- Relationship: Many-to-One
- Join Condition:
  `repo_transactions.collateral_security_id = investment_securities.security_id`
- Purpose: Audit credit eligibility, haircuts, and ISIN registrations on repos.

JOIN PATH: Accounts & Intraday Liquidity Events (Anomalous Drawdown Auditing)
- Relationship: One-to-Many
- Join Condition:
  `accounts.account_id = intraday_liquidity_events.account_id`
- Purpose: Report peak drawdown events against corporate entity profiles.

5. DATA LINEAGE & TRANSFORMATION PIPELINES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This lineage catalog maps the data flow from source transaction platforms into
analytical stores and final business intelligence report briefs.

LINEAGE PIPE: Daily Concentration Sweep Lineage
1. Upstream Source System:
   - Platform: Core Banking Ledger / Subsidiary Clearing Nodes.
   - Captured Event: EOD Child Account balance sweeps.
   - Raw Fields: `source_acc`, `target_acc`, `amount_transferred`, `time_stamp`.
2. Intermediate Integration Layer:
   - Ingestion Job: `sweep_extractor_daily` (ETL).
   - Validation Rule: Verify child day-end balance resolves to target floor cushion.
   - Target Schema: Committed directly to `sweep_executions`.
3. Downstream Reporting Layer:
   - Aggregation Job: `harvested_liquidity_summary`.
   - Business KPI: Total Harvested Yield / Concentration Performance.
   - Target UI View: Business Suite - Dashboard Tiles & Conversational BI.

LINEAGE PIPE: Nostro Settlement Exception Lineage
1. Upstream Source System:
   - Platform: SWIFT Messaging Gateway (Correspondent Accounts).
   - Captured Event: Daily SWIFT MT940 / MT950 Customer Statements.
   - Raw Fields: `correspondent_balance`, `value_date`, `account_iban`.
2. Intermediate Integration Layer:
   - Reconciliation Engine: `nostro_reconciler_pro` (ETL).
   - Validation Rule: Matches correspondent balance against internal ERP ledger balance.
   - Target Schema: Committed directly to `nostro_balances`.
3. Downstream Reporting Layer:
   - Alert Generator: `nostro_mismatch_alert_service`.
   - Business KPI: Variance Exception Amount / Reconciled Ratio.
   - Target UI View: Proactive Alerts Card (Critical vs Warning classification).

LINEAGE PIPE: Derivative MTM Revaluation Lineage
1. Upstream Source System:
   - Platform: Central CCP Clearing Interface (LCH / DTCC).
   - Captured Event: Daily Closing Yield Curve & Mark-to-Market Valuation.
   - Raw Fields: `contract_id`, `mtm_valuation`, `variation_margin_requirement`.
2. Intermediate Integration Layer:
   - Valuation Engine: `derivative_mtm_runner` (ETL).
   - Validation Rule: Mark-to-market revaluations must update in real-time.
   - Target Schema: Committed to `interest_rate_swaps.mark_to_market_value`.
3. Downstream Reporting Layer:
   - Risk Engine: `hedging_exposure_monitor`.
   - Business KPI: Net Open Derivative Risk Exposure / Variation Margin Call.
   - Target UI View: Analyst Actions - Root Cause Analysis / Recommend Actions.

6. CORE ANALYTICAL FORMULAS (PURE BUSINESS RULES)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The following logical rules are hardcoded into the platform's calculation
capabilities. Analytical agents must ground calculations in these strict schemas.

RULE: High-Quality Liquid Asset Stock Compilation
- HQLA_Stock = (HQLA_Level_1_Value) + (0.85 * HQLA_Level_2a_Value) + (0.50 * HQLA_Level_2b_Value)
- Cap Rules:
  - Level 2A + Level 2B HQLA must never exceed 40% of the total HQLA pool.
  - Level 2B HQLA must never exceed 15% of the total HQLA pool.
- Target Output: Sourced in `liquidity_buffers` database views.

RULE: Daily Nostro Balance Reconciliation Mismatch
- Variance = | correspondent_ledger_balance - internal_ledger_balance |
- Action Logic:
  - IF Variance > $10,000 AND Variance <= $100,000: Set status = 'Mismatch', Alert = 'Warning'.
  - IF Variance > $100,000: Set status = 'Mismatch', Alert = 'Critical'.
  - IF Variance <= $10,000: Set status = 'Reconciled', Alert = 'None'.
- Target Output: Populates `nostro_balances.reconciliation_status`.

RULE: Expected Variation Margin Call Allocation
- Variation_Margin_Call = Day_T_MTM_Value - Day_T-1_MTM_Value
- Action Logic:
  - IF Variation_Margin_Call is negative: AIM must post collateral to CCP before 10:00 EST.
  - IF Variation_Margin_Call is positive: Counterparty must post collateral to AIM.
- Target Output: Populates `collateral_positions.market_value`.

7. ESCALATION WORKFLOW SCHEMAS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Operational workflows routing data triggers are governed by these schema pathways.

PATHWAY: Intraday Drawdown Breaches
