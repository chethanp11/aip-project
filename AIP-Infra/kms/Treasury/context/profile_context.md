# Treasury Business Context

## Business Scope
Treasury manages enterprise liquidity, funding, cash positioning, collateral, market exposures, and balance-sheet resiliency. This context is directional metadata only. It must not be treated as factual business data; all balances, counts, rates, dates, and metric values must come from querying `treasurydb`.

## Authorized Users
- `Treasury_Analyst`
- `Treasury_SME`

## Authorized Domains
- Treasury & Capital Management
- Cash Management
- Liquidity Management
- Funding and Balance Sheet Management
- Collateral and Market Exposure Oversight

## Data Authority Rule
Use this context to understand table purpose, joins, and analytical intent. Do not answer factual questions from this file. Query `treasurydb` for all facts.

## Table Metadata

### `accounts`
- Business meaning: Customer and internal account balances used for Treasury liquidity, deposit, loan, and branch-level balance-sheet analysis.
- Typical use: deposit/loan totals, account-type mix, currency distribution, branch concentration.
- Key concepts: account type, branch, currency, balance, customer/account ownership.
- Common joins: `transactions` by account identifier where available.

### `transactions`
- Business meaning: Account activity and movement records used to understand cash flow behavior.
- Typical use: inflow/outflow analysis, volume trends, transaction behavior, account activity detail.
- Key concepts: transaction date, transaction type, amount, currency, account reference.
- Common joins: `accounts` by account identifier where available.

### `liquidity_buffers`
- Business meaning: Inventory of liquidity buffer assets and HQLA-style reserve instruments.
- Typical use: liquidity coverage analysis, buffer composition, haircut treatment, yield view.
- Key concepts: asset type, liquidity category, haircut, yield, eligible amount.
- Analytical caution: amounts and rates must come only from the database.

### `liquidity_sweeps`
- Business meaning: Cash movement instructions and sweep arrangements across operating and concentration accounts.
- Typical use: cash concentration, funding optimization, operating cash movement review.
- Key concepts: source account, target account, sweep threshold, sweep cadence, sweep rule.

### `sweep_executions`
- Business meaning: Execution events generated by cash sweep arrangements.
- Typical use: actual sweep movement analysis, exception review, settlement monitoring.
- Key concepts: sweep identifier, execution timestamp, moved amount, execution status.

### `treasury_positions`
- Business meaning: Treasury desk positions across liquidity portfolio, cash management, and investment portfolio holdings.
- Typical use: position inventory, market value exposure, liquidity bucket view, risk-weight categorization.
- Key concepts: desk, instrument type, currency, notional, market value, liquidity bucket, risk weight.

### `cash_forecasts`
- Business meaning: Forward-looking cash position projections by branch or operating unit.
- Typical use: short-term liquidity planning, projected inflows/outflows, closing balance forecast.
- Key concepts: forecast date, branch, currency, opening balance, projected inflows, projected outflows, confidence level.

### `funding_plans`
- Business meaning: Planned and executed funding actions across funding sources and tenor buckets.
- Typical use: funding gap management, plan execution tracking, cost-of-funds monitoring.
- Key concepts: funding source, tenor bucket, currency, planned amount, executed amount, target rate, status.

### `collateral_positions`
- Business meaning: Collateral inventory available or pledged for secured funding, clearing, or central bank facilities.
- Typical use: eligible collateral analysis, haircut treatment, pledged versus available collateral review.
- Key concepts: counterparty, collateral type, currency, market value, eligible value, haircut, pledged status.

### `fx_exposures`
- Business meaning: Foreign exchange net open positions and hedge coverage by currency pair.
- Typical use: FX exposure monitoring, hedge ratio review, limit surveillance.
- Key concepts: business date, currency pair, net open position, value-at-risk, hedge ratio, exposure status.

### `interest_rate_swaps`
- Business meaning: Interest-rate derivative positions used for asset-liability and rate-risk management.
- Typical use: hedge inventory, maturity profile, market value review, pay/receive exposure analysis.
- Key concepts: counterparty, notional, currency, pay leg, receive leg, maturity date, market value.

### `investment_securities`
- Business meaning: Treasury investment portfolio securities and accounting classifications.
- Typical use: book versus fair value review, maturity ladder, security-type concentration.
- Key concepts: issuer, security type, currency, par value, book value, fair value, maturity, classification.

### `intraday_liquidity_events`
- Business meaning: Intraday payment-rail liquidity movements and settlement queue observations.
- Typical use: intraday liquidity monitoring, payment rail outflow/inflow review, operational issue detection.
- Key concepts: event timestamp, payment rail, currency, inflow, outflow, queue status, operational note.

### `nostro_balances`
- Business meaning: Correspondent bank balances held for settlement and operating liquidity.
- Typical use: available cash monitoring, minimum operating balance checks, currency settlement readiness.
- Key concepts: correspondent bank, masked account, currency, balance date, ledger balance, available balance, minimum balance.

### `repo_transactions`
- Business meaning: Repurchase and reverse-repurchase secured funding transactions.
- Typical use: secured funding analysis, collateral funding cost, maturity ladder review.
- Key concepts: counterparty, direction, collateral type, currency, cash amount, repo rate, start date, maturity date.

### `stress_test_scenarios`
- Business meaning: Treasury liquidity stress scenarios and modeled survival horizon outputs.
- Typical use: stress testing, scenario comparison, liquidity breach monitoring.
- Key concepts: scenario name, run date, horizon, stressed outflow, stressed inflow, survival horizon, breach indicator.
